Au Gratin Potatoes

Home

Description

Au gratin potatoes are a French classic dish made by stacking thinly sliced potatoes in a baking dish, layering them with cheese and cream, and then baking until golden and bubbly. The dish is often topped with breadcrumbs for added texture.

This recipe offers the best way to make the easiest, cheesiest and most delicious au gratin potatoes with Gruyere.

Ingredients

  • 3 pounds Yukon Gold potatoes, peeled and thinly sliced
  • 1 cup heavy cream, divided
  • freshly grated nutmeg to taste
  • 2 cups shredded Gruyere cheese, divided
  • 3/4 teaspoon salt
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • freshly ground black pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
  2. Place the potatoes, garlic, and salt into a large pot;with enough water to cover. Bring to a boil; reduce heat to medium and simmer potatoes until tender but not mushy, 8 to 10 minutes. Use a slotted spoon to transfer about half the potatoes into a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  3. Pour 1/2 of the cream over the potatoes and season with black pepper and nutmeg; sprinkle 1/2 of the Gruyère cheese on top. Layer the remaining potatoes on top and season with black pepper and nutmeg. Pour over remaining cream and sprinkle with remaining Gruyère cheese.
  4. Bake in the preheated oven until cheese is golden brown on top, about 1 hour.
